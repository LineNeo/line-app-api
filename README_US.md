For teams that still depend on the Chrome version, web access, browser automation, or PC-based LINE sessions for customer service, private traffic operations, account hosting, and message synchronization, this is the right time to prepare a more sustainable replacement.

We provide a commercial LINE mobile protocol solution designed for private deployment, multi-account hosting, and business-system integration. It covers account registration, primary-device migration, primary/secondary multi-device access, message sending, contact management, group chat operations, message synchronization, webhook callbacks, and related operational capabilities.

This is not just a login utility or a browser automation replacement. It is a business-ready LINE API / LINE Protocol solution for teams that need long-term account operations, lower resource usage, and more control over their deployment environment.

## Core Capabilities

### Account Registration

Registration is one of the most important capabilities of this solution. Customers do not need to rely only on imported accounts or QR login. New accounts can be initialized through the registration flow and then moved into managed hosting.

This is valuable when:

- A new business needs to prepare LINE accounts in batches.
- The customer wants account creation to be connected to a unified internal system.
- Registration, login, hosting, synchronization, and messaging should be managed as one flow.
- The team wants to reduce manual device handling and manage the account lifecycle through software.

For customers that need account creation to become part of a repeatable business workflow, mobile-side registration and lifecycle management are important commercial capabilities.

### Primary-Device Access And Account Migration

The solution explicitly supports primary-device scenarios, not only secondary-device login. Primary-device support is critical for customers that need full account lifecycle management, existing-account migration, and workflows closer to real mobile-device usage.

Supported scenarios include:

- iOS primary-device access
- Android primary-device access
- Primary-device account migration
- Moving existing accounts into a new managed device system
- Running primary and secondary devices as part of a multi-device access strategy

These are the scenarios where primary-device and migration capabilities matter most: customers can move from older devices or legacy access methods into a managed system without treating every account as a one-off manual setup.

### Multi-Device Online Access

The solution supports both primary and secondary device access, making it suitable for different operational models:

- Primary devices: suitable for registration, migration, full account hosting, and lifecycle control.
- Secondary devices: suitable when the original main device should coexist with managed access.
- Multi-account hosting: suitable for customer service teams, private traffic operations, overseas businesses, and account-matrix systems.

This gives customers the flexibility to choose the access model that best matches their operational requirements, account risk profile, and migration plan.

### Messaging And Operations

For daily operations, the solution covers the capabilities customers normally need around messaging and account management:

- Sending text, image, video, audio, file, sticker, and contact-card messages
- Webhook callbacks for new messages, read receipts, revokes, chat changes, and login status
- Message history, incremental synchronization, and media download
- Contact profile, friend relationship, block, favorite, hidden status, and alias management
- Group creation, member invitation, member removal, leaving groups, and notification settings
- Profile, avatar, home background, contact QR ticket, and related account operations
- Sticker store, channel information, and end-to-end encryption related capabilities

- More complete account lifecycle: covers registration, migration, login, hosting, synchronization, and messaging.
